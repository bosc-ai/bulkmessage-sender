1.1 — 1.2 · The building blocks

From a chat app to a business channel

You already know WhatsApp the phone app. A business at scale uses something different underneath — and that difference explains almost every rule in this guide.

The app you know vs. the Business API

The green WhatsApp app on your phone is built for one person chatting with friends. It can’t safely send thousands of messages, be shared by a team, or connect to your website and spreadsheets.

The WhatsApp Business API (also called the Cloud API) is Meta’s professional version of WhatsApp. There is no app to open — instead, a tool like Weflux plugs into it and gives your whole team one shared screen. It’s what powers verified business chats, broadcasts, and automations.

API, in one line

An API is just a doorway that lets two pieces of software talk. The WhatsApp API is the doorway between Weflux and Meta’s WhatsApp network. You never see it — Weflux handles it for you.

So what is a “BSP”?

A BSP (Business Solution Provider), also called a Tech Provider, is a Meta-approved company that hosts the API connection on your behalf. Meta doesn’t hand the raw API to every small business — you go through a BSP.

Weflux is your BSP. It holds the approved connection to Meta, keeps it healthy, and turns the technical machinery into simple screens: an inbox, a broadcast button, a flow builder.

How a message actually travels

You & your team
Type a reply, or launch a broadcast, inside Weflux.
Weflux (your BSP)
Holds your approved connection and speaks the API for you.
Meta WhatsApp Cloud API
Meta’s global network delivers it to the phone.
Your customer’s WhatsApp
Lands as a normal WhatsApp message — blue ticks and all.
It’s two-way

When the customer replies, Meta pushes it straight back through the same path into your Weflux inbox — instantly. The technical name for that push is a webhook.

Why the rules exist

Because you’re messaging through Meta’s official channel, Meta sets the rules — the 24-hour window, template approval, opt-outs, quality ratings. Weflux doesn’t invent these; it simply keeps you on the right side of them.

1.3 — 1.4 · The rules that shape everything

The 24-hour window & templates

This is the single most important idea in the whole product. Understand it once and half of Weflux’s behaviour stops being surprising.

The 24-hour customer-service window

Meta protects people from spam with a simple rule: a business may send free, normal messages only within 24 hours of the customer’s last message. Every time a customer messages you, a fresh 24-hour timer starts. Inside that window you can type anything — answers, images, menus. Once it closes, free-form messages are blocked.

In Weflux you never have to calculate this. A coloured banner sits at the top of every chat:

  • A green “Session open — reply freely for another 16h 20m” banner means the window is open.
  • An amber “24h window closed — use a template to re-engage” banner means it has expired, and the reply box is replaced by a Send Template button.
You always start with a template

To message someone first — a brand-new lead, or a customer who’s gone quiet — you must use a template. Free typing is only for replying inside an open window.

So what is a “template”?

A template is a pre-written message that Meta has reviewed and approved in advance. Because Meta already checked it, you’re allowed to send it any time — even outside the 24-hour window, even to someone who’s never messaged you.

Templates can hold blanks called variables, written {{1}}, {{2}}, that you fill with real details (a name, an order number) at send time — so “Hi {{1}}, your order {{2}} shipped” becomes personal for each person.

The three template categories

MARKETING
Promotions, offers, re-engagement. Most expensive; needs opt-in; subject to marketing limits.
UTILITY
Order updates, receipts, reminders tied to a specific action. Cheap, approved fast, reaches anyone anytime.
AUTHENTICATION
One-time passcodes and verification codes only.
Category matters

Order updates should be UTILITY — they cost less and slip past the 24-hour window. Save MARKETING for genuine promotions. Weflux’s e-commerce template library picks the right category for you (Chapter 9).

1.5 · Permission, reputation & cost

Staying healthy — and what it costs

Three last foundations: getting permission to message, keeping your number’s reputation high, and understanding who charges you for what.

Opt-in & opt-out

Opt-in means the customer agreed to hear from you (ticked a box, messaged you first, shared their number knowingly). WhatsApp marketing is permission-based — you should only broadcast to people who opted in.

Opt-out is the reverse: if someone no longer wants messages, you must stop. Weflux handles this two ways — a per-contact opt-out toggle, and opt-out keywords (like “STOP”) that unsubscribe a contact automatically when they text them. Broadcasts exclude opted-out people by default.

Quality rating & messaging limits

Meta grades each number with a quality rating — GREEN YELLOW RED — based on how people react to your messages. Too many blocks or “report spam” taps and it drops.

Your number also has a daily messaging limit — how many new people you can message in 24 hours. New numbers start at 250/day and climb automatically (1K → 10K → 100K → unlimited) as you verify your business and send good-quality messages.

Protect your rating

Only message people who want it, keep templates useful, and honour opt-outs. A RED rating can pause your sending entirely.

What it costs — and who charges you

Here’s the part that surprises people: Weflux does not charge you per message. Meta bills your own WhatsApp payment method directly, and Weflux adds ₹0 markup. You pay Meta’s price, nothing more.

4.2 · Three ways to organise

Tags, stages & custom fields

Beginners mix these three up constantly. They do very different jobs — learn the difference once and your data stays clean.

Tag

A free-form label like VIP or Urgent. A contact can hold many. Used to group and filter. You add and remove them yourself.

Stage

Where a contact sits in your funnel. A contact has exactly one at a time: New Lead → Contacted → Qualified → Opportunity → Customer → Churned.

Custom field

An extra profile detail you invent — “Company”, “Account size” — with a type (Text, Number, Date, Yes/No, URL). Defined once, filled per contact.

Where you set them up

  • Tags — create reusable, colour-coded tags in Settings → Tags; apply them from a contact’s Info panel, the Add-Contact form, or a CSV.
  • Stages — rename and recolour the seven built-in stages in Settings → Contact Stages to match your own wording.
  • Custom fields — define them in Settings → Custom Attributes; the values appear in each contact’s Info panel.
Good to know

Custom Attributes are available on every plan. Tags and Contact Stages management appears on paid plans (tags still auto-create when you import).

Glossary

Every term used in this guide, in plain English. When something on screen confuses you, it’s probably defined here.

24-hour window — The period after a customer’s last message during which you can send free-form replies. Once it closes, only approved templates get through.

Add-on — A module (CRM, Tasks, Calls, Migrate) switched on for your account by the Weflux team, separate from your plan tier.

API — A doorway that lets two pieces of software talk. The WhatsApp API is the doorway between Weflux and Meta’s WhatsApp network.

Approved template — A pre-written message Meta has reviewed and allowed. Only approved templates can start or re-open a conversation.

Assignment — The agent who “owns” a conversation. The first agent to reply is auto-assigned; anyone can reassign it.

Broadcast / Campaign — One approved template sent to many contacts at once.

BSP (Business Solution Provider) / Tech Provider — A Meta-approved company that hosts your WhatsApp API connection. Weflux is your BSP.

Canned response — A saved reply snippet inserted by typing / in a chat.

Category — A template’s type: Marketing, Utility, or Authentication. It sets the price and the sending rules.

Cloud API — Meta’s hosted version of the WhatsApp Business API that Weflux connects to.

Connection — A WhatsApp Business number linked to Weflux.

Contact — A person in your address book, identified by phone number.

CRM / Deal — Your sales pipeline. A deal is one opportunity with a value and a stage (Lead → Won/Lost).

Custom attribute (field) — An extra profile field you define (Company, Account size) shown on each contact.

Embedded Signup — Meta’s official pop-up where you log in and pick your number in one click. Weflux never sees your password.

Entitlements — The live list of what your account is allowed to use; upgrades apply within ~30 seconds.

Label — A chip attached to a single conversation (versus a tag, which attaches to the contact).

Lead journey — The timeline of every conversation a contact has ever had with you.

Lifecycle stage — Where a contact sits in your funnel: New Lead → Contacted → Qualified → Opportunity → Customer → Churned.

Messaging limit / tier — How many new people you can message in 24 hours. New numbers start at 250/day and scale up.

Opt-in / opt-out — Permission to message someone / their request to stop. Opted-out contacts are excluded from broadcasts.

Phone Number ID · WABA ID — Meta’s identifiers for your number and your WhatsApp Business Account. The one-click path fetches them for you.

Quality rating — Meta’s health score for your number (Green / Yellow / Red), based on how people react to your messages.

Reply flow — An automatic follow-up template that fires when someone replies to a specific campaign.

Session — Another name for an open 24-hour window with a customer.

Tag — A reusable, colour-coded label on a contact (VIP, Urgent). A contact can hold many.

Template — See Approved template. Holds variables like {{1}} filled at send time.

Variable {{n}} — A numbered blank in a template filled with real data (name, order number) when you send.

Webhook — An instant “something happened” push — Meta uses one to deliver replies to your inbox; your store uses one to fire order updates.

Workspace / Organization — Your Weflux account. The person who registers is its Admin.
