Cookie Policy

Last updated: 18 May 2026 — How WaChat uses cookies and your choices.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the site remember preferences, keep you logged in, and understand how visitors use the site.

How WaChat uses cookies

WaChat uses cookies on this marketing website (wachat.serves.in) and in the WaChat application (wachats.serves.in). Cookies in the application are required for the service to function.

Cookie categories

Essential cookies (always active)

These cookies are required for the site and application to function. They cannot be disabled.

  • session_id — Keeps you authenticated in the WaChat dashboard. Expires when you close your browser or after 24 hours of inactivity.
  • csrf_token — Prevents cross-site request forgery attacks. Session-scoped.
  • pref — Remembers your UI preferences. Expires after 1 year.

Analytics cookies (optional)

We use privacy-friendly analytics to understand how visitors use the site. These cookies do not track you across other websites and contain no personally identifiable information.

  • _plausible — Plausible Analytics. No personal data, no cross-site tracking, GDPR-compliant by default.

Functional cookies (optional)

  • wa_chat_widget — Remembers your chat widget conversation state. Session-scoped.

What we do NOT use

  • No advertising or retargeting cookies
  • No Facebook Pixel, Google Ads, or similar trackers
  • No selling or sharing of cookie data with advertisers

Managing cookies

You can control non-essential cookies using the cookie banner shown on your first visit. You can also manage cookies in your browser settings — note that disabling essential cookies will prevent login and core features from working.

Browser guides: Chrome · Firefox · Safari

Changes

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in the cookies we use or for legal and operational reasons. We will notify you of material changes through the cookie banner.
