WhatsApp broadcasts are the highest-engagement channel most Indian businesses have — open rates routinely beat email by 5–10×. But a careless blast can get your number rate-limited or your quality rating dropped. Here's how to do it right.

![image.png](blob:https:/app.pagescms.org/7e80c72e-d6bb-4e30-8266-d80d24d9c933)

## 1. Start from an opted-in list

Only message people who have opted in. Import your contacts, tag them by source, and keep your opt-out handling clean. A smaller engaged list beats a large cold one every time.

## 2. Use the right template category

- **Marketing** — promotions, offers, re-engagement.
- **Utility** — order updates, reminders, account notices.
- **Authentication** — one-time passcodes.

Sending a promo as a utility template is the fastest way to get a template paused.

## 3. Write for one person

Personalise the first line with the contact's name and a specific reason for the message. Keep it short, lead with the value, and include a single clear call to action.

## 4. Throttle and watch quality

Ramp volume gradually on a new number. Watch your delivery and read rates, and pause if quality dips. BulkMessageSender shows these live so you can react before Meta does.
